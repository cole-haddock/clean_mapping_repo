Intervention Zone:

Oakland's Encampment Abatement Policy divides the city into 
high-sensitivity and low-sensitivity zones. High sensitivity
zones (locations near critical infrastructure, such
as traffic lanes and schools) make up more than 90% of the city. 
In "Low-Sensitivity Zones," encampments can stay if they 
meet strict city requirements. 

For further information, please read Swept Off the Map. 


Grants Pass v. Johnson:

The Supreme Court’s City of Grants Pass v. Johnson (2024) 
ruling allowed state and local governments to cite, arrest,
or evict unhoused people for living in public without first 
offering them housing. After the ruling was announced,
Governor Gavin Newsom urged California officials to push forward 
with aggressive encampment clearances, saying, “Time to do your job.”

For further information, please read Swept Off the Map.


Intervention Types:

There are five types of interventions: Full Closures, Health &
Hygiene interventions, Deep Cleanings, Partial Closures and 
Emergency Closures. Since 2024, the City of Oakland has focused almost 
entirely on full closures.

For further information, please read Swept Off the Map.
